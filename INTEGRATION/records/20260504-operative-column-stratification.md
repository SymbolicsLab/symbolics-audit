### Six structural sub-findings (each confirmed across the walk)

1. **Slot-type framework**: ascensions have constitutive-active + anticipatory-captured; in-level slots have operative-active + stable-captured. Both rows differ at slot-types. Confirmed across all 5 in-level slots and all 4+1 ascension-shaped events.

2. **Distinct affective signature per ascension**: sameness-insight (A(L1→L2)) / connection-insight (A(L2→L3)) / variation-insight (A(L3→L4)) / ownership-insight (A(L4→L5+)) / plastic-act affect (the "+"). Each tracks the operation-kind coming online.

3. **Memory reorganization at ascensions**: causal-trace → instance-trace → kind-trace → inference-trace → modal-trace → ownership-trace. Plus reorganization-within-ownership-trace at the "+". Retroactive at each ascension.

4. **Forward-orientation reorganization symmetric with memory**: causal-readiness → cut-readiness → kind-readiness → inferential-readiness → modal projection → identity-projection. Modal projection is L4-specific form of universal forward-orientation, parallel to how kind-readiness is L2-specific. Expands the May 3 modal-projection thread from one register to two parallel ones.

5. **Compound affect tracks captured-row richness**: bare at L1 → richest at L5 → reorganized at "+". Affect's compound structure tracks the captured row's richness at each level.

6. **Second-body necessity reduces to entanglement at A(L4→L5+)**: J-shape (strong at L1 / A(L1→L2); diminishing through A(L2→L3) / A(L3→L4); returning at A(L4→L5+) and after) reduces to the entanglement principle — at slots where self IS the cut, other must be present as constitutive complement. "No self without other" is the structural reason. The J-shape is what entanglement produces when applied to the slot-content of the apparatus's slots.

### Deeper structural insight: finite-then-open architecture

The apparatus has a finite vertical ascent (four ascensions producing five levels) but open horizontal re-foldability at L5+ (no upper bound on internal ascensions via the "+"). Once reflexivity is in place at A(L4→L5+), the agent can refold indefinitely on her own apparatus. There is no L6 because there is no further operation-kind to introduce beyond reflexivity. What there is, is iteration of reflexivity on itself — kairos moments, plastic acts, type-space reorganizations. The apparatus is structurally **finite-then-open**.

This is what permanent T-failure produces operationally: not closure, not termination, but ongoing re-organizability. The structural-transcendental commitment cashed out at the architecture level.

## Methodological notes

- **Four-register integration as anti-bloodless commitment.** At each operative slot, the operative cells were named alongside affect, memory, and forward-orientation registers. This prevented the bloodless-machinery failure mode and exposed structural patterns (compound affect, memory reorganization, forward-orientation symmetric with memory) that would have been invisible without the integration.

- **Level-specific naming foregrounded.** After L1/A(L1→L2) walks, user caught a phrasing thinness (using "distinction at L2" rather than "form-recognition firing"). Subsequent slots used level-specific structural-angle names: cut firing / kind-formation fold firing / form-recognition firing / etc. The column-identity (distinction-as-structural-angle) holds abstractly, but each slot's specific shape was named explicitly.

- **Simple exemplars throughout.** Per the methodological commitment from the May 3 record, exemplars stayed simple (toddler with spider/bus/keys; paw print → tiger near; Helen Keller; hunter; chess player). Modus ponens IS paw print → tiger near; the formal logician's articulation comes later as L3 captured-row codification.

- **Alternating level-ascension order.** Decided after L1: walk in level-ascension-level-ascension order rather than all levels first then all ascensions. Reason: the ascension's dynamics are most visible right after the prior level is freshly worked (the pressure-toward-next is freshly named); the ascension's content IS the prior level, so we have what we need; this follows the apparatus's own bottom-up directionality.

- **L5 then "+" separately.** User's call: walk L5 in-level first, then treat the "+" separately as an ascension-shaped event. This kept the L5-in-level operation distinct from the L5-internal-revision-mechanism (plastic act / kairos). Worked cleanly.
