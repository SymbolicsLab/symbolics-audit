## What was integrated

The 2026-05-02 morning metabolism stratification described the metabolic cycle as remainder-driven. Late in that session, the user surfaced that the cycle has an inside-driver missing from the integration: items wrongly *included* in the cut whose actual structure presses for expulsion (the poisonous mushroom currently classified as food). Settled on **intrusion** as the term; decision was locked, vault application deferred. This record covers the deferred application plus the structural tightening that came with it — anchoring metabolism to its drivers rather than letting it float.

**Load-bearing claim landed:** Metabolism is not a free-floating concept. The cycle turns because every cut generates two structural pressures, and metabolism is the system's response to them. Without remainder + intrusion, no cycle.

## Key claims

1. **Two drivers**:
   - **Remainder** = outside-driver. What the cut excludes, pressing inward. *Bulk concept*: most silent (field-excess), some pressing (foreclosed/adjacent — see `03_Phenomenology §Three registers of remainder`).
   - **Intrusion** = inside-driver. Items currently classified inside the cut whose actual structure presses for expulsion. *Pressing-edge concept without a bulk*: the cut's interior is mostly correctly-classified content not generating pressure.

2. **The asymmetry is structural**, not a naming-artifact. Remainder is bulk-with-pressing-edge; intrusion is pressing-edge-without-bulk. The naming asymmetry tracks the structural fact. (Mirrors the Hannover anomaly/remainder asymmetry: anomaly is contingent, remainder is structural; intrusion sits in anomaly's position on the inside-side.)

3. **The 4-mode space** becomes legible by combining the two modes (updating/contesting from the morning's metabolism stratification) with the two drivers:

| | Remainder-driven | Intrusion-driven |
|---|---|---|
| Updating (pre-L4 capable) | Expansion (extend coverage) | Contraction (trim coverage) |
| Contesting (L4+) | Expansion (admit new kind) | Expulsion (purge wrongly-typed kind) |

Examples grounded in the table within the vault entries:
- **Updating-by-expansion**: toddler adds tiger to danger; bird's startle threshold drifts; clinician's category-boundary widens.
- **Updating-by-contraction**: operant unlearning; clinician retracts a diagnostic application; threshold drift away from disconfirmed items.
- **Contesting-by-expansion**: DSM adds disorder category; physics extends to relativistic phenomena; legal systems incorporate new rights-bearers.
- **Contesting-by-expulsion**: chemistry purges phlogiston; DSM removes homosexuality; bibliography retracts fabricated reference; programme dissolution.

4. **Paradigm-shifts-that-expel are structurally distinct from paradigm-shifts-that-expand.** Both are L4+ contesting, but they differ in which driver dominates: the apparatus had something it shouldn't (intrusion-driven) vs. lacked something it needed (remainder-driven). Conflating them obscures what each kind of revision does.

5. **The structural axis added to the pathology grid**: failure can be located by which 4-mode cell the system fails to operate in. Asymmetry-of-driver-response (e.g., a system capable of updating-by-expansion but not updating-by-contraction) is itself a pathology shape.
