## Self-Individuation — Advances integrated

### 1. Functional vs. recognitive normativity (NEW — major)
Two independent normative standards. Functional = success/survival (one-body). Recognitive = identity/recognition (two-body). They don't covary: optimal survival ≠ correct individuation. This is what makes the two-body condition constitutive, not facilitative.
- **Added to**: Vault 01_Ontology, new subsection "Two kinds of normativity" in Two-Body section

### 2. Private self argument (NEW)
Parallel to Wittgenstein's private language: a privately constituted self-individuation is impossible because there's no standard of identity, only habit. Without external normative friction, "whatever the system does to maintain itself" is all that selfhood consists in — and that's not a norm, it's a pattern.
- **Added to**: Vault 01_Ontology, within "Two kinds of normativity"

### 3. Proleptic attribution (noted but not added to vault)
The caregiver treats the infant's behavior as normatively structured before it is. Normativity lives in the dyad. Important for the developmental story but more application than theory.

## Social Science Understanding — Advances integrated

### 4. Remainder metabolism = law/rule gradient applied to the classified (NEW — derived from existing theory)
What happens to remainder depends on where the classified entities sit on the law/rule gradient. Inert (rocks) → causally active (ecosystems) → adaptive (bacteria) → strategic/narrative (humans). This is NOT a new axis — it's the law/rule gradient doing double duty. During integration, conversation with user established that "domain continuity" is not an independent dimension: the field is always continuous, and apparent joints are high-resilience index fits, not domain properties.
- **Added to**: Vault 03_Phenomenology, new subsection "Remainder metabolism" before "Remainder in social systems"

### 5. Structural debt (NEW)
Every working stabilization accrues remainder invisible to administrators but experienced at boundaries. The debt is empirical — as boundary populations reorganize, the domain shifts in ways the model can't detect. A stabilization that can't register its own debt is empirically wrong about the system.
- **Added to**: Vault 03_Phenomenology, within "Remainder in social systems"

### 6. Positional opacity (NEW — extends existing concept)
Each revision sees its predecessor's failures clearly and its own dimly. Opacity at the institutional level: asymmetric access to the index of the classification, distributed across positions.
- **Added to**: Vault 03_Phenomenology, within "Remainder in social systems"

### 7. Understanding as stabilization (noted but not added to vault)
Social-scientific understanding = stabilization under feedback, not convergence. Important epistemological application but domain-specific rather than foundational.

### 8. Narrative specificity / misrecognition (noted but not added to vault)
Social science classifies self-narrating subjects. Remainder experienced as misrecognition. Important but domain-specific.

## Theory decision during integration

**The field is always continuous.** Apparent joints are high-resilience index fits, not domain properties. "Domain continuity" is not an independent dimension — it collapses into fit/resilience (04_Ecology). This means remainder metabolism is fully derivable from existing theory: how much remainder = fit/resilience; what happens to it = law/rule gradient applied to classified. No new axis needed. The Social Science paper's Level 1/Level 2 binary was a coarse-grained simplification for its audience.
