## Summary

Three vault-development integrations executed in one day, all triggered by the Synthese Free Will draft (deadline 2026-05-15) and all closing the same diagnostic frame: **substance-ontology over-reach via captured-row semantics applied to active-row phenomena**. The three integrations are distinct categories of error, not one. Each was developed in conversation with the user pressing on a specific weak point in the Free Will paper, and each landed in the vault with cross-references to the others.

The unified diagnosis: the libertarian's strongest opponents (Mele, van Inwagen, Pereboom) all rely on substance-ontology presuppositions that read active-row phenomena (the firing, the actualization, the present-moment self-application) through captured-row semantics (state-form, cross-world stipulability, probability-over-events). Processual ontology refuses these presuppositions; the objections lose their target.

The session also made the **positive position explicit**: processual libertarianism is a fifth position alongside event-causal, agent-causal, non-causal, and criterial-causation libertarianisms. The four classical positions accept the substance-ontology frame and try to satisfy the triple constraint (alternatives + sourcehood + up-to-meness) within it; that is why Pereboom can find a category-by-category objection to each. Processual libertarianism rejects the frame.

## What was done

### Integration 1 — Recognition required at the self-applicative fold (morning)

**Trigger.** User pressed on §6 of the Free Will draft: the entanglement reduction (*self IS the cut, so other must be present as constitutive complement*) was carrying its argumentative weight by parallel structure with the metabolism-environment cut, but didn't articulate why the parallel holds asymmetrically. Why must the self-applicative cut's complement be a *recognizing* other rather than the physical environment that already serves as the metabolism cut's complement?

**Argument added.** At the self-applicative fold:
1. What's maintained is a normative claim ("this apparatus, as I am applying it, is mine; the application is appropriate to who I am"), not a causal configuration. Operates in the register of correctness/misuse, not survival/homeostasis.
2. The field is normatively silent on self-application: causal feedback registers first-order success/failure but cannot register correctness/incorrectness of self-application, because the standard for correct self-application is identity-relative, not causal-functional.
3. A self-applicative system with only field-feedback collapses correctness into success — paranoid system at the self-applicative scale, but here as structural failure rather than empirical drift.
4. Therefore the self-applicative cut's complement must be recognizing — what holds the cut in place has moved from causal interaction to normative ascription.

This is the private-language argument generalized to the reflexive node. Closes the gap on the entanglement reduction's argumentative grounding.

**Mid-edit tweak applied.** User caught the "fitness-claims" framing (cells exhibit fitness; they don't make claims about it) and asked to reformulate the contrast as causal-functional outcomes (survival/homeostasis) vs. normative validity (correctness/appropriateness). Applied throughout.

### Integration 2 — Stipulability asymmetry: wake fixable, firing not (afternoon)

**Trigger.** User pressed on §5's reply to Mele's contrast argument. The current §5 reply rejects substance ontology (no agent-substance whose post-*t* behavior needs accounting) but doesn't yet handle the steel-manned reviewer move: Mele can grant processual ontology and re-pose the argument at the process level — "two processes with histories identical up to *t*, going different ways at *t*; without a contrastive explanation, still luck."

**Argument added.** The active/captured distinction in the 2×3 grid (May 2-3 work) is not just a temporal-mode marker; it has a structural consequence for cross-world identity stipulation. **Captured-row content** (representation, meaning, commitment, the wake of past actualizations) admits cross-world identity stipulation — captured content has state-form sufficient for identity criteria because persistence requires partial-articulation, and partial-articulation supplies identity conditions. **Active-row content** (distinction-firing, mattering-firing, actualization-firing) does NOT admit cross-world identity stipulation — active content has no state-form prior to its firing, because firing is its mode of existence; to "fix the active firing at *t* across worlds" is to fix the firing itself, but the firing IS what individuates the present moment.

**Application: Mele dissolved.** Mele's "agent identical up to *t*, different selection at *t*" stipulates captured-row content (the wake), which is stipulable, and asks what accounts for active-row difference (the firing), which is not stipulable cross-world. The cross-world difference IS accounted for by the agent's active firing — by the agent in the present-moment-instantiation mode. The luck-judgment itself is malformed: "luck" requires firing-as-event-happening-*to*-agent semantics; the active row has firing-as-agent-happening. *Calling the active firing luck would be calling agency itself luck.* The libertarian luck-argument is a category error — substance-ontology stipulability semantics applied to active-row content.

### Integration 3 — Pereboom dissolution: within-world variant (later)

**Trigger.** Held thread from Integration 2: whether the dissolution extends cleanly to Pereboom's contrastive-explanation argument in §6, or whether Pereboom's setup needs its own treatment. Provisional read at end of Integration 2: same move applies, but not fully worked through.

**Argument added.** Pereboom's settling/disappearing-agent argument is the within-world variant of the same substance-ontology error. Where Mele trades on cross-world identity stipulation, Pereboom trades on within-world contrastive explanation: in event-causal libertarianism, antecedent events render the decision probable but not settled; the decision happens (A rather than B); demand a contrastive explanation; the libertarian's options each fail; the agent disappears.

The hidden premise carrying the argument is the **chance-event picture**: the firing at *t* is an event with objective probability given antecedents; probability < 1 means antecedents don't fully determine the outcome, so something else must "fill the gap" between probable and actual. This is captured-row reading of active-row content. The wake conditions admissibility but does not generate a probability distribution; the firing IS the actualization, not a sample. Strip the chance-event picture and the settling-question evaporates.

**Where Pereboom is more demanding than Mele.** Pereboom's within-world version permits a residual deflation-pressure that Mele's cross-world version doesn't: *"OK, the firing is the agent's actualization. But then your 'agency' is just whatever happens to fire."* This pressure is blocked by the entanglement reduction at the self-applicative fold (Integration 1) — recognition constitutes the firing as agent-firing rather than mere event. Agency has constitutive structure (recursive, self-applicative, recognitively-anchored) that distinguishes it from generic indeterministic firings.

**The Pereboom dissolution requires both moves**: (1) reject chance-event picture (within-world application of stipulability asymmetry); (2) entanglement reduction makes firing agent-firing. Mele needed only (1).

**Positive position made explicit.** User asked "what is Pereboom arguing for? Randomness?" then clarified "I meant in the libertarian position itself" — which surfaced the four classical libertarianisms and led to the explicit articulation of processual libertarianism as a fifth position. Each classical position accepts the substance-ontology frame and tries to satisfy the triple constraint (alternatives + sourcehood + up-to-meness) within it; that is why Pereboom can find a category-by-category objection to each. Processual libertarianism rejects the frame.

## Methodological notes

- **Each integration started with a Free Will paper weak-point and ended with a vault subsection that closes the gap structurally.** The pattern: user identifies the prose-level weakness; conversation derives the structural argument; vault gets the new subsection; cross-references propagate. Same pattern across all three.

- **Each subsection went into the structurally appropriate vault location, not just "wherever Mele/Pereboom reply lives."** The recognition argument went into 01_Ontology §Constitution vs. Operation (because it generalizes beyond the Free Will context and has standalone structural payload). The stipulability asymmetry went into 01_Ontology §The Unified Emergence (because it's a general structural feature of the 2×3 grid). The Mele application went into 02_Operations §Commitment Corollaries' adjacent space (because it's where actualization/commitment lives). The Pereboom application went into the same §Wake and firing subsection as #### (because it's the within-world variant of the same dissolution).

- **The May 2-3 row-correction work (active = actualization, captured = commitment) was load-bearing for all three integrations.** The 2×3 grid as description-machinery + the active/captured row-identity is what made the structural arguments possible. This validates the May 3 correction as a foundational move.

- **The "fitness-claims" tweak revealed a methodological pattern**: when an argument relies on a contrast between active-row and captured-row phenomena, framing must avoid attributing captured-row activities (claim-making, stipulating, settling) to entities that don't have them at the lower scale. The recognition argument was about distinguishing causal-functional outcomes from normative claims; framing those outcomes as "fitness-claims" would have introduced a claim-attribution where none was needed.
