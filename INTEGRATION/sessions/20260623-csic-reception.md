## The day, in order (PB's account, verbatim sense)

PB spoke fourth — after the keynote and three other participants.

**1. The pre-talk reframe (during Talk 3's Q&A).** The third speaker had proposed *injecting friction into AI output* (PB's aside: unrealistic, but still). The Q&A turned into a multi-minute debate over whether that was a good idea — the worry being that injected friction would be **synthetic / artificial**. After several minutes PB raised his hand and said he **disagreed with everything that had been said**. His inversion:

> The synthetic/artificial output *is the smooth AI output* — it's what removes the actual friction from the real world. A full response would carry nuance, contradictions, tensions — which is exactly what the AI takes away. A friction-*full* output would be the authentic one.

The room went **speechless but agreeing**. He had reorganized the whole conversation around his distinction *before presenting a word of his own talk*.

**2. His talk + Q&A.** Q&A enthusiastic. "Everyone loved the idea, like a lot. Everyone."

**3. The one-hour roundtable.** ~**95% of it orbited his idea.** Challenges came mostly at the **implementation** level; he "had responses for everything." The conversation went deep — **moral, ethical, political.** One striking thread: someone wondered whether **governments would actually want kids who think critically** (i.e. the political economy of friction-restoring education).

**4. The ~5% that wasn't his idea — the Dennett move.** Others kept invoking **"mind"** and **Dennett's intentional stance** in relation to affect. PB said that didn't seem like the right debate: if you're going to bring Dennett into AI-affect questions, the relevant idea is his **self as a center of narrative gravity** — and on *that* view, **AIs do have selves, and that's the problematic part.** Room baffled again. An older philosopher charged him with **anthropomorphizing.** PB's rebuttal: the point is that **people don't care anymore** — he agreed *from an essence perspective*, but **Dennett's narrative-gravity self still holds for AI.** The philosopher **respected the rebuttal.**

**5. Clowes (leaving).** The keynote, **Robert W. Clowes** (companion-AI; Bochum / IfilNOVA), expressed **keen interest in collaborating** — possibly on **his current MIT Press project.** Clowes finds his own current work **too pessimistic**, and sees PB's approach as bringing a **real, optimistic, practical angle** to the piece.

**6. Organizers.** PB also read the **organizers as keenly interested** in his idea. "We'll see where that goes." (His room-reads are a calibrated instrument — [[user_social_perception_trust]].)
